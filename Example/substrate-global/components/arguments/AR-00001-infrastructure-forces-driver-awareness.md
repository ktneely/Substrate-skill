# Infrastructure Changes Force Driver Awareness

## Summary

Traffic calming features like parklets, chicanes, and optical illusions are not dangerous obstructions but deliberate design elements that force drivers out of mental autopilot into conscious thought, making them aware of their surroundings and driving more safely.

## Details

The argument counters the common complaint that traffic calming infrastructure is dangerous. Parklets and chicanes do not cause accidents — they prevent them by forcing drivers to pay attention. When a driver crashes into a traffic island at speed, the fault lies with the dangerous driver, not the infrastructure designed to slow them. The infrastructure's purpose is to create discomfort that triggers conscious thought: "Oh, I'm in a residential area, there are kids, I need to slow down." This is the mechanism by which traffic calming achieves its safety goals.
