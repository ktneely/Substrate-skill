# 70% Insect Volume Decline

## Summary

70% of the volume of insects has been lost, with disastrous consequences for ecosystems including bird populations and food production ability, yet this decline is largely invisible due to baseline syndrome.

## Details

The massive decline in insect populations occurs gradually year over year, making it imperceptible to individual observers. People see fewer insects in gardens and fewer dead bugs on windscreens and assume this is normal because it matches their lifetime experience. The consequences cascade through ecosystems: bird numbers decline due to lack of food, and food production ability is threatened due to loss of pollination. This fact is used as a primary example of baseline syndrome in action.
