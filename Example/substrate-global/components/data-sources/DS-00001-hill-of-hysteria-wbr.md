# The 'Hill of Hysteria' – Why Changing Our Environment Is So Hard

## Summary

A blog article from the Wandsworth Bridge Road Association (October 2024) explaining psychological barriers to urban environmental change, introducing concepts of Baseline Syndrome, Motonormativity, and the "Hill of Hysteria" response curve. Argues that car dependency is a cultural addiction requiring conscious intervention through urban redesign.

## Details

The article examines why changes to urban environments generate anxiety and hostility despite evidence that the status quo is failing. It introduces several psychological frameworks: Baseline Syndrome (accepting current degraded environments as normal), Motonormativity (excusing dangerous driving behaviour as socially normal), and the Hill of Hysteria (the predictable pattern of resistance then acceptance following environmental changes). Uses case studies from Ljubljana, Helsinki, Oslo, and Amsterdam to demonstrate successful car-free transitions. Updated November 2024 with new research data.
