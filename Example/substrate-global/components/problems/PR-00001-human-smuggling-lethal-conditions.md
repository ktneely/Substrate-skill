# Human Smuggling Under Lethal Conditions

## Summary

Human smuggling along the US-Mexico border operates under conditions that routinely kill migrants. Smugglers pack people into sealed vehicles with no ventilation, water, or food, leading to deaths from suffocation, hyperthermia, and dehydration. The San Antonio disaster is the most extreme example but represents a pattern of lethal smuggling practices.

## Details

The human smuggling trade along the US-Mexico border has become increasingly lethal as enforcement has intensified. Smugglers (coyotes), increasingly controlled by cartels like Cártel del Noreste, pack migrants into sealed vehicles — tractor-trailers, vans, and other enclosed spaces — to avoid detection at checkpoints and border patrols. These vehicles have no ventilation, no air conditioning, no water, and no food. Migrants are packed tightly together in extreme heat, leading to deaths from suffocation and hyperthermia. The San Antonio disaster of June 27, 2022, which killed 53 migrants, is the deadliest single incident but represents an ongoing pattern. The problem is driven by the financial incentives of the smuggling trade, cartel control of routes, and enforcement policies that push migrants into dangerous crossing methods.
