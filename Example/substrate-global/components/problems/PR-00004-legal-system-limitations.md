# Legal System Limitations in Smuggling Prosecutions

## Summary

Despite the severity of the San Antonio disaster, the legal system faces significant limitations in prosecuting smuggling cases. Cartel leaders often remain insulated from prosecution, while lower-level participants (drivers, mechanics, recruiters) bear the brunt of legal consequences.

## Details

The criminal prosecutions following the San Antonio disaster resulted in charges against the driver (Homero Zamorano Jr), the mechanic (Juan D'Luna Bilbao), recruiters (Christian Martinez), and smugglers (Felipe Orduña Torres, José Martínez Olvera). However, the cartel leadership that ultimately profits from and directs the smuggling operations often remains insulated from prosecution. The legal system struggles to connect lower-level participants to the broader criminal organization. Additionally, the complexity of cross-border investigations, witness intimidation, and the difficulty of gathering evidence in smuggling operations all limit the effectiveness of prosecutions. The result is that while individual participants may be convicted, the organized criminal enterprise continues to operate.
