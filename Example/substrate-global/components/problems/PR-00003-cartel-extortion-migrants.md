# Cartel Extortion of Migrants

## Summary

Mexican cartels control human smuggling routes and extort migrants at every stage of the journey. Migrants pay thousands of dollars for passage that is often lethal, with cartels treating human lives as a revenue stream with no regard for safety.

## Details

Cartels like Cártel del Noreste control the smuggling routes along the US-Mexico border and treat human smuggling as a business. They charge migrants $5,000 to $15,000 or more per person for passage, with payments collected at various stages. Cartels tax independent smugglers who want to use their routes, creating a monopoly on the trade. The enormous financial incentive — billions of dollars annually — drives cartels to continue operations despite lethal outcomes. Migrants have no recourse when things go wrong; they cannot report abuse to authorities because they are themselves in violation of immigration law. The cartel control of smuggling transforms what might be independent, small-scale operations into organized criminal enterprises with the capacity to move large numbers of people in lethal conditions.
