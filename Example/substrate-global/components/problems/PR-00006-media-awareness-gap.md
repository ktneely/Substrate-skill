# Media Awareness Gap

## Summary

Despite the severity of the San Antonio disaster, public awareness of human smuggling conditions remains limited. Comprehensive journalism like Elliott Woods' Texas Monthly article came over three years after the event, indicating a gap in sustained media coverage of smuggling-related deaths.

## Details

The San Antonio disaster of June 27, 2022, received initial media attention but the deeper story — the cartel connections, the smuggling economics, the survivor testimonies, the family trauma — was not comprehensively documented until Elliott Woods' article "Stay Strong, My Brother" was published in Texas Monthly in December 2025, over three years later. This gap reflects a broader pattern: human smuggling deaths receive brief news coverage but rarely sustained investigative journalism. The lack of sustained media attention means the public does not fully understand the scope of the problem, the cartel involvement, or the policy failures that contribute to lethal conditions. This awareness gap reduces political pressure for meaningful solutions.
