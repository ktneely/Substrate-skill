# Car Addiction as Cultural Dependency

## Summary

A framing that treats car dependency not as a rational necessity but as a cultural addiction built over decades through advertising, infrastructure design, and social norms — requiring treatment-style intervention rather than accommodation.

## Details

This frame reframes the debate from "cars vs no cars" to "addiction vs recovery." Car dependency developed over decades through advertising, cultural references, assumed aspirations, and environment design that further encouraged car use. Like all addictions, car-dependent societies struggle to admit the problem and constantly seek to excuse the behaviour. The frame draws parallels with smoking cessation: in 1950, 80% of UK smoked; today only 12%, and public smoking is socially unacceptable. The same cultural shift is needed for car use — "think before we drive."
