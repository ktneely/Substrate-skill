# Hill of Hysteria (Change Response Curve)

## Summary

A model describing the predictable pattern of public response to urban environmental changes: intense anxiety and hostility during implementation, followed by adaptation and eventual acceptance once the change becomes the new baseline.

## Details

The "Hill of Hysteria" describes how each manipulation of the urban environment triggers a predictable cycle: initial resistance and anxiety, peak hostility during implementation, gradual adaptation, and eventual acceptance where people no longer want to return to the previous state. This pattern repeats for every change — parklets, cycle lanes, pedestrianisation — yet each time the outcome is positive once the new baseline is established. The model explains why evidence of post-change acceptance does not prevent resistance to the next change.
