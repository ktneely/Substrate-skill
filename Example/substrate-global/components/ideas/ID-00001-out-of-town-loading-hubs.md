# Out-of-Town Loading Hubs for Urban Delivery

## Summary

An idea for replacing HGV and van access to city centres with out-of-town consolidation hubs where goods are transferred to cargo-bikes for last-mile delivery, reducing pollution, congestion, and road safety risks.

## Details

The concept involves establishing loading and consolidation facilities outside urban centres where HGV deliveries are received. Goods are then sorted and delivered to city businesses via cargo-bikes or other low-impact transport modes. This model already exists in practice — Oxford colleges use a cargo-bike delivery firm to consolidate 100+ daily deliveries into once-daily cargo-bike routes. Benefits include massive reduction in van journeys, lower pollution, reduced congestion, improved road safety, and decreased carbon emissions. Alternative models include collection hubs and last-mile delivery services.
