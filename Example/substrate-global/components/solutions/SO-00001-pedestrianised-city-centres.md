# Pedestrianised City Centres

## Summary

Redesigning civic space away from car priority toward people-oriented environments with pedestrianised streets, cycle lanes, trees, and extended public transport — proven to increase liveability and business success.

## Details

Pedestrianisation removes vehicles from urban centres, replacing road space with people-friendly infrastructure: trees, cycle lanes, public squares, and enhanced public transport. Initial resistance is predictable (Hill of Hysteria) but post-implementation acceptance is near-universal. Case studies from Ljubljana (97% support after implementation, up from under 40% before), Amsterdam (1971-2020 transformation), Helsinki, and Oslo demonstrate consistent success. Benefits include safer environments, improved mental and physical health, quieter calmer spaces, local mobility, small commutes, and connected communities.
