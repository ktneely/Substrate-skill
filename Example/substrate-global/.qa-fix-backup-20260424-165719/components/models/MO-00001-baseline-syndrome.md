# Baseline Syndrome (Shifting Baseline)

## Summary

A psychological phenomenon where each generation accepts the degraded state of their environment as normal, because it is what they have always known. This prevents people from recognising environmental decline and the urgency for action.

## Details

Baseline Syndrome (also called Shifting Baseline Syndrome) explains why humans integrate their experienced environment into their psyche as the "normal" state. Year-on-year degradation becomes invisible because the change is gradual. For example, people observe fewer insects and assume this is normal because it matches their lifetime experience, unaware that 70% of insect volume has been lost. The same mechanism applies to urban design — people accept car-dominated streets as normal because that is all they have known.
