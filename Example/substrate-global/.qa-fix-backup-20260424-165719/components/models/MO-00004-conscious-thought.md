# Conscious Thought (Dual-Process Driving Behaviour)

## Summary

A psychological model explaining that drivers operate on mental autopilot by default, and that unexpected urban features (parklets, chicanes, optical illusions) force drivers into conscious thought mode, making them aware of their environment and driving more safely.

## Details

Most driving behaviour is automatic and unconscious — drivers follow routines without actively processing their surroundings. Urban design features that disrupt expectations — things in the road, odd road layouts, optical illusion crossings — force drivers out of autopilot and into conscious thought. This deliberate discomfort is the mechanism by which traffic calming works: it makes drivers pay attention to pedestrians, cyclists, and the residential nature of the area. The model supports the argument that infrastructure should prioritise forcing awareness over making driving easier.
