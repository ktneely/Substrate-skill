# Body Identification and Repatriation

## Summary

Following the June 27, 2022 San Antonio disaster, a complex international process of identifying and repatriating the remains of 53 deceased migrants was undertaken. Families from Mexico, Guatemala, and Honduras traveled to Texas to identify their loved ones and arrange for remains to be returned home.

## Details

After the San Antonio disaster, authorities faced the challenge of identifying 53 deceased migrants from three countries: Mexico (26), Guatemala (21), and Honduras (6). The Bexar County Medical Examiner's Office conducted autopsies and forensic identification. Families traveled from their home countries to San Antonio to identify remains through dental records, DNA testing, and personal effects. The repatriation process involved coordination between US authorities and the governments of Mexico, Guatemala, and Honduras. Families like those of Delfina Bacajol, Virgilia López, Luz Estrella Cuevas Remolino, and María Antonia Torres Morales endured the additional trauma of traveling to identify and repatriate their loved ones.
