# Ljubljana City Centre Pedestrianisation

## Summary

In 2007, Ljubljana, Slovenia pedestrianised its entire city centre despite less than 40% public support and the mayor being slapped by an angry voter. The transformation included tree planting, cycle lane construction, and public transport extension, resulting in one of Europe's most liveable cities.

## Details

The pedestrianisation of Ljubljana's city centre in 2007 is a landmark case study in urban transformation. Mayor Zoran Janković proposed the changes with minimal public support (under 40%) and faced intense opposition including physical assault during the campaign. The implementation included: full city centre pedestrianisation, tree planting, cycle lane construction, and public transport extension. The outcome: Ljubljana became known as one of Europe's most liveable cities, businesses flourished, the mayor won every subsequent election, and a survey showed 97% of residents now support keeping the streets car-free. This case perfectly illustrates the Hill of Hysteria cycle.
