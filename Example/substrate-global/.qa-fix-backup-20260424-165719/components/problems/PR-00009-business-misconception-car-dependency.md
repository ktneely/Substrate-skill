# Business Misconception About Car Dependency

## Summary

The persistent myth that businesses can only flourish next to busy roads full of vehicles, despite evidence that people shop (not cars), and that pedestrian-friendly environments increase business success.

## Details

Businesses massively over-estimate how many customers arrive by car. Vehicles are metal boxes being driven by people going elsewhere — they are not entering shops. European city centres flourish with pedestrianised streets and squares, yet in the UK the same changes are resisted based on the belief they will "punish" businesses. The reality is that better environments for people lead to more successful shops. This misconception is one of the biggest barriers to urban transformation and is repeatedly disproven once changes are implemented and the Hill of Hysteria cycle completes.
