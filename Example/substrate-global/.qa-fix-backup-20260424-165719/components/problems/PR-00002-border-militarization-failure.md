# Border Militarization Failure

## Summary

US border enforcement policies, particularly "Prevention Through Deterrence," have failed to stop migration while making the journey more dangerous. Fortification of urban crossing areas funneled migrants into remote terrain and increased reliance on professional smugglers, worsening outcomes.

## Details

The US strategy of "Prevention Through Deterrence," implemented in the 1990s, fortified urban border crossing areas with walls, fences, and increased patrols. The intent was to deter migration by making crossing more difficult. Instead, the policy funneled migrants into remote, harsh desert terrain where the environment itself became a lethal barrier. More critically, it made independent crossing nearly impossible, forcing migrants to rely on professional smugglers. This increased the power, profitability, and organization of smuggling networks — including cartels like Cártel del Noreste. The policy created the conditions for disasters like San Antonio by incentivizing smugglers to use sealed vehicles to bypass checkpoints. Despite decades of increasing border enforcement spending, migration continues and has become more dangerous.
