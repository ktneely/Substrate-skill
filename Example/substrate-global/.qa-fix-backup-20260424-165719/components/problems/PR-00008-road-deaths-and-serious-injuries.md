# Road Deaths and Serious Injuries

## Summary

An average of 35 people per week (30,000 per year) are killed or seriously injured on UK roads, with a fifth being pedestrians. Society accepts this toll in a way it would never accept in other industries such as aviation.

## Details

The airline industry operates with a zero-death tolerance, yet road transport kills and seriously injures 35 people weekly in the UK. A fifth of these are pedestrians. This acceptance stems from motonormativity — the subconscious excusing of driver behaviour because car use is so ingrained in the collective psyche. People assume road risks "just have to be" accepted because they cannot envision a safe alternative. The implicit acknowledgment that anyone could be both a victim and a perpetrator of injury leads to subliminal excusing of drivers.
