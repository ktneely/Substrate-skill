# Urban Space Dominated by Cars

## Summary

Over 90% of public realm in cities is allocated to moving and storing private vehicles, squeezing pedestrians, cyclists, wheelchairs, bins, trees, seating, and all other uses into the remaining 10%, creating tension and dysfunction in urban spaces.

## Details

The private car is the most space-inefficient form of transport yet consumes the vast majority of public space. In London, road space equals the equivalent of 10 Hyde Parks. Everything else — pedestrians, buggies, wheelchairs, bins, bike parking, EV chargers, trees, seating — must compete for the remaining 10%. This creates huge tension and the perception that "there's no space for change." The problem is compounded by induced demand: building more road capacity creates more cars, longer journeys, and perceived need for yet more capacity — a vicious circle that worsens quality of life through stressful commutes, road rage, disconnection from neighbours, isolation, and poor health.
