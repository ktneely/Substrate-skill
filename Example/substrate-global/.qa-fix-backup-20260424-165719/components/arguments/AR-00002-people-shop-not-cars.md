# People Shop Not Cars

## Summary

The economic argument that pedestrian-friendly environments increase business success because people (not cars) are the customers, and businesses massively over-estimate how many customers arrive by car.

## Details

This argument dismantles the primary business objection to pedestrianisation. Vehicles are metal boxes being driven by people going elsewhere — they do not enter shops. European city centres demonstrate that pedestrianised streets and squares support thriving businesses. The Living Streets "Pedestrian Pound" research provides empirical evidence that businesses over-estimate car-based customer arrivals. The better the environment for people, the more successful the shops. This argument is essential for overcoming business resistance to urban transformation.
