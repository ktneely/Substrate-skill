# Zoran Janković

## Summary

Mayor of Ljubljana, Slovenia who pedestrianised the city centre in 2007 despite initial opposition including being slapped by an angry voter, and has won every election since.

## Details

Zoran Janković was the mayor of Ljubljana who proposed and implemented the pedestrianisation of the city centre in 2007. Less than 40% of citizens initially supported the proposal, and he was famously slapped by an angry voter during the campaign. Despite this intense opposition (the Hill of Hysteria), the changes were implemented: trees planted, cycle lanes built, public transport extended. Today Ljubljana is one of Europe's most liveable cities, businesses flourish, and Janković has won every election since. A survey shows 97% of residents now support keeping the streets car-free.
