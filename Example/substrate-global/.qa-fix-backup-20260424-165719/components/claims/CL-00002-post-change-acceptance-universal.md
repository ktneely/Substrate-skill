# Post-Change Acceptance Is Universal

## Summary

Once urban environmental changes are established and people adapt to the new baseline, acceptance is near-universal and no one wants to return to the previous state — despite intense resistance during implementation.

## Details

This claim asserts a consistent pattern across all urban environmental changes: intense resistance during the "Hill of Hysteria" implementation phase, followed by adaptation and eventual acceptance where the change becomes the new normal. Evidence includes Ljubljana's shift from under 40% support to 97% support after pedestrianisation, Amsterdam's transformation from 1971 car-dominated streets to 2020 people-friendly city, and Helsinki/Oslo achieving zero pedestrian deaths. The claim is supported by decades of research showing that people love changes once established and do not want to go back.
