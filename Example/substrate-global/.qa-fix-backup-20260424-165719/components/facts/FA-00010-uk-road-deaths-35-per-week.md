# UK Road Deaths 35 Per Week

## Summary

An average of 35 people per week (30,000 per year) are killed or seriously injured on UK roads, with a fifth of these being pedestrians.

## Details

This statistic represents the annual toll of road casualties classified as killed or seriously injured (KSI) in the UK. A fifth (approximately 6,000) are pedestrians. The figure is used to highlight the double standard in acceptable risk between road transport and other industries such as aviation, which operates with near-zero death tolerance. The acceptance of this toll is attributed to motonormativity — the subconscious excusing of dangerous driving behaviour.
