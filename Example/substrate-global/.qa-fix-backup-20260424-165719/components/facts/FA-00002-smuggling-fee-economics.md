# Smuggling Fee Economics

## Summary

Migrants pay thousands of dollars to smugglers (coyotes) for passage from Central America and Mexico through the US border. The smuggling trade generates billions in revenue for cartels like Cártel del Noreste, creating a powerful financial incentive to continue operations despite lethal conditions.

## Details

The human smuggling trade is a multi-billion dollar industry. Migrants from Central America and Mexico pay smugglers anywhere from $5,000 to $15,000 or more per person for passage to the United States. These fees are collected at various stages of the journey, with different smugglers handling different segments. The revenue flows to cartels like Cártel del Noreste, which control the smuggling routes and tax independent operators. The enormous financial incentive drives cartels to continue smuggling operations despite the lethal conditions they create for migrants.
