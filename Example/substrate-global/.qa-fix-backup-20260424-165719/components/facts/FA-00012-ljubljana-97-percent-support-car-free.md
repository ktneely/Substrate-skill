# Ljubljana 97% Support Car-Free After Pedestrianisation

## Summary

When Ljubljana's mayor proposed pedestrianising the city centre in 2007, less than 40% of citizens were in favour and he was slapped by an angry voter. After implementation, a survey showed 97% of residents support keeping the streets car-free.

## Details

Ljubljana, capital of Slovenia, provides a dramatic example of the Hill of Hysteria cycle. In 2007, the mayor proposed full city centre pedestrianisation with less than 40% public support. He was famously slapped by an angry voter during the campaign. Despite this, the city centre was pedestrianised: trees planted, cycle lanes built, public transport extended. Today Ljubljana is known as one of the most liveable cities in Europe, businesses flourish, and the mayor has won every election since. A recent survey showed 97% of residents support keeping the streets car-free — a complete reversal from initial opposition.
