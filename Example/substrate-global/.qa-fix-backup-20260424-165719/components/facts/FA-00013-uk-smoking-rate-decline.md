# UK Smoking Rate Declined From 80% to 12%

## Summary

In 1950, approximately 80% of UK adults smoked; today the rate is around 12%, and smoking in public has become socially unacceptable — demonstrating that massive cultural shifts in behaviour are possible.

## Details

The decline in UK smoking rates from approximately 80% in 1950 to around 12% today serves as a parallel for the cultural shift needed regarding car use. The change came through a combination of health evidence, legislation (public smoking bans), and social norm transformation. Smoking in public, once completely normal, is now socially unacceptable. This fact is used to counter the argument that car dependency cannot change — if smoking culture can transform this dramatically, car culture can too. The tobacco industry's resistance to change mirrors the vested interests opposing car-use reduction.
