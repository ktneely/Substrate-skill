# Prevention Through Deterrence Policy

## Summary

US Border Patrol's "Prevention Through Deterrence" strategy, implemented in the 1990s, funneled migration into remote and dangerous terrain, inadvertently increasing reliance on professional smugglers and contributing to lethal conditions like those in the San Antonio disaster.

## Details

Prevention Through Deterrence was a US Border Patrol strategy implemented in the 1990s that fortified urban border crossing areas, intentionally funneling migrants into remote, harsh terrain where the environment itself would serve as a deterrent. The policy had the unintended consequence of making independent border crossing nearly impossible, forcing migrants to rely on professional smugglers. This increased the power and profitability of smuggling organizations like Cártel del Noreste. The policy is widely cited by researchers and journalists as a contributing factor to the rise in lethal smuggling operations.
